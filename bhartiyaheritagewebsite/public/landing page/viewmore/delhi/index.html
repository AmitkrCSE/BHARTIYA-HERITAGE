Exploring the Traditions of Delhi.

The Red Fort is a 17th-century Mughal fortress built in towering red sandstone, now a complex of museums. It was the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal dynasty for nearly 200 years, until 1857. It is located in Old Delhi

Visit Red Fort

Architecture and Key Features:

Here's a breakdown of its key architectural features:

  • Red Sandstone: As the name suggests, the fort is primarily constructed from red sandstone, giving it a striking appearance.
  • Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience): This open hall was used for meeting with officials and the public. It features an elevated marble throne for the emperor.
  • Walls and Gates: The fort is surrounded by massive walls with several impressive gates, including the Lahore Gate and the Delhi Gate.
  • Mughal Dynasty: The Red Fort served as the principal residence of Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years, from Shah Jahan to Bahadur Shah II.
  • Independence Day: The Indian flag is hoisted every year at the Red Fort on Independence Day (August 15th) by the Prime Minister of India.

List of Temples in Delhi.

  • Akshardham Temple

    The Akshardham Temple in Delhi is a sprawling complex showcasing traditional Indian architecture, art, culture, and spirituality. Dedicated to Swaminarayan, it features intricate carvings, exhibitions, and a boat ride depicting Indian history.

    View More

  • Laxminarayan Temple

    The Laxminarayan Temple, also known as Birla Mandir, is a prominent Hindu temple in Delhi dedicated to Vishnu and Lakshmi. Built by the Birla family, it's known for its beautiful architecture, intricate carvings, and serene atmosphere.

    View More

  • Hanuman Mandir

    Hanuman Mandirs (temples) across India are dedicated to Lord Hanuman, the Hindu god of strength, devotion, and courage. They serve as places of worship, community gathering, and recitation of the Ramayana, honoring Hanuman's unwavering devotion to Lord Rama.

    View More

  • Kalkaji temple

    The Kalkaji Temple in Delhi is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Goddess Kali, a fierce form of Durga. It's an ancient temple, believed to house a self-manifested idol of the goddess, attracting devotees seeking blessings and wish fulfillment.

    View More

  • Nili Chhatri

    The Nili Chhatri Temple in Delhi, near Nigambodh Ghat, is an ancient Shiva temple believed to be established by the Pandava king Yudhishthira. Its name, meaning "blue umbrella," likely refers to a blue dome or tilework that once adorned it

    View More

  • Lotus Temple

    The Lotus Temple in Delhi is a Baháʼí House of Worship known for its distinctive lotus-like shape. It's a symbol of unity and welcomes people of all religions to meditate and pray.
